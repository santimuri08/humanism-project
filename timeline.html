Design History Timeline

Trace the evolution of Humanist Modernism from its roots to today's digital implementation.

1920s - Bauhaus Foundation

The Bauhaus school revolutionized design education, establishing "form follows function" as a core principle.

1950s - Swiss Typography

The International Typographic Style emerged, emphasizing grid systems and clean, objective layouts.

1957 - Helvetica Born

Max Miedinger created Helvetica, one of the world's most widely used typefaces.

1970s - Humanist Revival

Adrian Frutiger developed highly legible typefaces, prioritizing human readability over pure geometry.

1980s - Digital Revolution

Desktop publishing democratized design, bringing professional tools to everyone.

1990s - Web Typography

Design adapted to screen-based reading, facing new challenges of resolution and browser compatibility.

2000s - Web Fonts Emerge

@font-face and services like Google Fonts freed designers from system font limitations.

2010s - Responsive Design

Mobile-first thinking reshaped web typography for all screen sizes.
