About This Project

This website is both a demonstration and implementation of Humanist Modernism design principles, created for IS 373 at New Jersey Institute of Technology.

What is Humanist Modernism?

Humanist Modernism is a design philosophy that combines the clarity and functionality of modernist design with the warmth and readability of humanist typography. It prioritizes the reader's experience above all else.

Design Principles in Action

1. Optimal Reading Typography

Implementation: 19px body text, 1.75 line-height, maximum 720px line width (~65 characters).

Why it matters: Research shows this combination reduces eye strain and increases comprehension during extended reading.

2. Generous Whitespace

Implementation: Modular spacing scale (8px, 16px, 24px, 40px, 64px) applied consistently.

Why it matters: Proper spacing creates visual breathing room and helps establish clear content hierarchy.

3. Warm, Sophisticated Palette

Implementation: Muted blues (#2C5F8D), warm grays (#6B7B8C), and subtle accents (#D4A574).

Why it matters: Colors set the emotional toneâ€”warm hues create a welcoming, comfortable environment.

4. Clear Visual Hierarchy

Implementation: Size and weight differentiation, not decorative elements.

Why it matters: Users can scan and understand content structure at a glance.

5. Accessibility First

Implementation: WCAG AAA compliant, semantic HTML, ARIA labels.

Why it matters: Design should be inclusive and usable by everyone.

Technologies Used

  • HTML5: Semantic markup for better accessibility and SEO
  • CSS3: Custom properties, Grid, Flexbox, and animations
  • JavaScript: Vanilla ES6+ for interactive features
  • React (Modern Version): Component-based architecture with Framer Motion
